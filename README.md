# Accident Analysis Dashboard - India

## Project Overview
This project presents an advanced-level Accident Analysis Dashboard built using data visualization tools to analyze road accidents across India. The dashboard provides deep insights into accident patterns, casualties, causes, geographic distribution, and influencing factors such as weather, road type, visibility, and time.

The objective is to support data-driven decision-making for improving road safety and reducing accident rates.

![Dashboard](https://github.com/Nagendra7290/Accident-Analysis-Dashboard---India/blob/main/Screenshot%202026-04-04%20014947.png)

---

## Objectives
- Analyze total accidents and casualties across India
- Identify major causes of accidents
- Understand state-wise and city-wise accident distribution
- Evaluate impact of external factors such as weather, visibility, and road type
- Detect temporal patterns (day, week, trends)
- Provide actionable insights for safety improvements

---

## Key Metrics
- Total Accidents: 200 Million
- Total Casualties: 35 Thousand

---

## Dashboard Components

### 1. Accident & Casualties by Cause
Analysis of key accident causes:
- Overspeeding contributes the highest number of accidents
- Distraction and poor road conditions also significantly impact accident rates
- Weather and drunk driving show relatively lower but notable contributions

Insight:
Overspeeding is the primary driver of accidents and requires strict enforcement.

---

### 2. Accident & Casualties by State and City
- Maharashtra and Tamil Nadu show the highest accident counts
- Cities like Chennai, Kolkata, and Chandigarh lead in accident frequency

Insight:
Urban regions with dense traffic show higher accident concentration.

---

### 3. Accident & Casualties by Festival
- Majority of accidents occur during non-festival periods
- Minimal variation observed during major festivals like Holi, Eid, Diwali, and New Year

Insight:
Daily traffic behavior impacts accidents more than seasonal events.

---

### 4. Accident & Casualties by Temperature
- Fluctuations observed across temperature ranges
- No strong linear correlation, but extreme conditions show instability

Insight:
Environmental conditions have moderate influence on accident frequency.

---

### 5. Accident & Casualties by Day of Week
- Accidents are relatively consistent throughout the week
- Slight peaks observed on weekends

Insight:
Weekend travel increases accident probability slightly.

---

### 6. Accident & Casualties by Road Type
- Urban roads have the highest accidents
- Highways show fewer accidents but potentially higher severity
- Rural areas have the lowest counts

Insight:
Urban congestion is a major contributor to accident frequency.

---

### 7. Accident & Casualties by Visibility
- Low visibility conditions lead to maximum accidents
- High visibility reduces accident occurrence significantly

Insight:
Visibility is a critical factor affecting road safety.

---

### 8. Accident Distribution by State
Top contributing states:
- Maharashtra (highest contribution)
- Tamil Nadu
- West Bengal
- Punjab
- Delhi
- Karnataka
- Telangana

Insight:
Industrial and high-population states show higher accident rates.

---

### 9. Casualties by State
- Casualty distribution closely follows accident distribution
- Maharashtra leads in both accidents and casualties

Insight:
Higher accident zones directly correlate with higher casualty rates.

---

### 10. Accident by City
Top cities:
- Chennai
- Kolkata
- Chandigarh
- Pune
- Mumbai
- Delhi
- Bangalore
- Hyderabad

Insight:
Metro cities dominate accident statistics due to traffic density.

---

## Data Analysis Approach
- Data Cleaning: Removed inconsistencies and standardized formats
- Data Modeling: Structured relationships between location, time, and accident factors
- Aggregation: Used sum of accident_id and casualties
- Visualization: Line charts, bar charts, and donut charts for comparative analysis

---

## Tools and Technologies
- Power BI for dashboard creation
- Excel/CSV for data preprocessing
- DAX for calculated measures
- Data modeling for relational insights

---

## Key Insights
- Overspeeding is the leading cause of accidents
- Urban areas are high-risk zones
- Visibility significantly impacts accident occurrence
- Festivals have minimal influence on accident trends
- Weekends show slightly higher accident activity

---

## Conclusion
The dashboard highlights that human behavior and urban congestion are the primary drivers of accidents in India. Strategic interventions such as speed control, better traffic management, and improved visibility conditions can significantly reduce accident rates.
